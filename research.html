Research

My research intertwines philosophy of physics, general philosophy of science, and metaphysics of science. I am mainly focused on four research areas.

[1] Inter-level relations in science

I’m interested in how scientific theories that describe reality at different levels are related, and in how best to model reduction and emergence in science, especially in physics. Among other topics, my work has focused on functional reduction and on developing this approach within the philosophy of science.

[2] Thermodynamics and statistical physics

I work on foundations of thermodynamics and statistical mechanics, especially the applicability of equilibrium concepts to unconventional domains such as gravitating systems.

[3] Spacetime theories

As part of SNF’s “Temporal Existence” project, I investigate the structure of spacetime theories and the metaphysics of time, focusing on Newton–Cartan theory and on relativistic/cosmological settings.

[4] Quantum mechanics

I work on the ontology of quantum mechanics, especially on the physical meaning of the wavefunction, and on structuralist and functionalist approaches to quantum theory.
